# MATH 360 — Probability and Statistics

**Prerequisites:** MATH 110

## Course Description

Reasoning under uncertainty: probability theory, random variables, common
distributions, estimation, and hypothesis testing — with a computational
flavor (simulation is a first-class tool, not just formulas). This course is
a hard prerequisite for machine learning and the honest interpretation of
any metric, benchmark, or A/B test you'll ever read.

## Learning Objectives

- Compute probabilities using counting, conditional probability, and Bayes'
  theorem — and avoid the classic fallacies.
- Work with random variables: expectation, variance, and the common
  distributions (Bernoulli, binomial, Poisson, uniform, normal,
  exponential).
- Explain the law of large numbers and the central limit theorem, and what
  they justify in practice.
- Estimate parameters (maximum likelihood, confidence intervals) and run
  and interpret hypothesis tests honestly (p-values, power, multiple
  comparisons).
- Use simulation (Monte Carlo) to answer probability questions that resist
  closed forms.

## Topic Outline

1. Probability foundations: sample spaces, events, axioms, counting.
2. Conditional probability, independence, Bayes' theorem; base-rate
   neglect and other traps.
3. Discrete random variables: PMFs, expectation, variance; Bernoulli,
   binomial, geometric, Poisson.
4. Continuous random variables: PDFs and CDFs; uniform, exponential,
   normal.
5. Joint distributions, covariance, correlation (and why correlation is
   not causation, mathematically).
6. The law of large numbers and the central limit theorem.
7. Monte Carlo simulation as a working tool.
8. Estimation: sampling distributions, maximum likelihood, confidence
   intervals.
9. Hypothesis testing: p-values, errors and power, A/B tests, the
   multiple-comparisons problem.
10. Linear regression and a first look at Bayesian inference.
