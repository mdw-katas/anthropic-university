# MATH 110 — Mathematical Foundations

**Prerequisites:** none

## Course Description

A refresher and on-ramp for a student 26 years removed from the math
classroom. Rebuilds fluency in algebra, functions, and mathematical notation,
then introduces the habits of mathematical thinking — precise definitions and
elementary proof — that the rest of the math track depends on. Replaces the
traditional calculus sequence in this degree; the essential calculus ideas
(limits, growth rates, derivatives as slopes) are surveyed in their final
unit so that later courses can lean on them.

## Learning Objectives

- Manipulate algebraic expressions confidently: exponents, logarithms,
  polynomials, inequalities.
- Read and write standard mathematical notation: sets, functions, sigma and
  pi notation, quantifiers.
- Understand functions deeply: domain, range, composition, inverses, and the
  major families (linear, polynomial, exponential, logarithmic).
- Compare growth rates of functions — the mathematical backbone of big-O
  analysis.
- Follow and construct simple direct proofs.

## Topic Outline

1. Numbers and algebra refresher: fractions, exponents, radicals, solving
   equations and inequalities.
2. Functions: definition, notation, graphs, composition, inverses.
3. Exponentials and logarithms; why log base 2 rules computer science.
4. Sequences, series, and sigma notation.
5. Sets and set notation; quantifiers ("for all", "there exists").
6. Growth of functions: comparing polynomial, exponential, and logarithmic
   growth; asymptotic intuition.
7. Mathematical reasoning: definitions, theorems, and direct proof.
8. Calculus survey: limits, the derivative as a rate of change, the integral
   as accumulation — enough to read texts that assume them.
